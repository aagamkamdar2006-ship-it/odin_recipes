Medu Wada

Description

Medu Wada is a savory South Indian fritter shaped like a doughnut, made from urad dal (black gram). Crispy on the outside and soft inside, it is a breakfast staple often served with sambar and coconut chutney.

Loved for its unique texture and flavor, Medu Wada is deep-fried and highly nutritious, providing plant-based protein. It's perfect as a snack or meal, especially when paired with hot, spicy sambar.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentil)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • Few curry leaves, chopped
  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Soak urad dal in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Drain and grind to a smooth, fluffy paste using as little water as possible.
  3. Add salt, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, and coriander; mix well.
  4. Heat oil for deep frying.
  5. Wet hands, shape batter into a ball, flatten, and make a hole in the center.
  6. Drop carefully into hot oil; fry until golden brown on all sides.
  7. Drain and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
